You need to complete a secure NHS online application for a Care ID.

You will need a Care ID if you are starting a new role or moving to a new NHS Trust.

A Care ID could be a card, security code, fob or device that will allow you to access:

  • national systems
  • clinical data
  • patient information

How to complete your application

The application has 2 parts:

1. Enter your personal information

Use a short online form to enter your personal details, including your National Insurance Number.

This must be completed by 9:02am Friday 29 July 2022.

2. Download the secure Verify Care ID app

When you have submitted your personal information, a link for the secure NHS Verify Care ID app will be sent to you by text message and email.

Use the Verify Care ID app to submit your documents and take a passport-style photo.

This must be completed within 15 days of receiving the link to the app.

Documents you need

You must provide 2 photo IDs and 1 proof of address.

2 photo IDs from this list:

  • UK or international passport
  • UK, EU or international driving licence
  • UK Biometric residence permit card
  • HM Armed Forces identity card

You will need original, physical documents and expired documents will not be accepted.

1 proof of address document from this list:

Documents from joint accounts with your name and the name of 1 or more account holders can be used.

  • bank account or credit card statement, this must be from the last 6 months
  • mortgage statement, this must be from the last 6 months
  • utility bill, this must be from the last 6 months
  • council tax statement, this must be from the last 12 months
  • UK driving licence (if you are using your driving licence as a photo ID you cannot use it as proof of address)

You will need statements or bills that have been sent to your address. Statements from online accounts will not be accepted.

You can ask your bank, building society or other service provider to send a statement to you or go to a branch and ask them to print and stamp your customer record.

Help and support with your application

Applying online and using the mobile app is the quickest way to verify your identity and get your Care ID.

Contact your manager, Registration Authority agent or application sponsor for help with your application.

Use the NHS Trust Registration Authority website to find your Registration Authority manager or agent.

Email enquiries@nhsdigital.nhs.uk for help and support using the website or app to verify your identity.
